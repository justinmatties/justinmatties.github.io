Master's Research

My master's research focused on investigating the biomechanical and physiological effects of advanced footwear technology, specifically carbon-plated running shoes ("super shoes"). This study looked into how different types of footwear affect running performance, energy expenditure, and injury prevention strategies.

Key Findings:

  • Exploring the energetic cost differences between super shoes and traditional footwear
  • Impact on running economy and gait biomechanics
  • Sex-specific responses to advanced footwear technology

Current Projects

My ongoing research continues to investigate individual biomechanical responses to different footwear and interventions, with a focus on injury prevention and optimizing athletic performance.

  • Exploring sex-specific biomechanical responses to advanced footwear technologies.
  • Using computational tools to analyze gait mechanics and improve injury prevention strategies.
  • Investigating the role of footwear in improving running performance across diverse populations.
