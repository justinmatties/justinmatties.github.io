About Me

Welcome! I’m Justin Matties, an applied biomechanics consultant with a Master’s in biomechanics and a PhD candidate at the University of Massachusetts. My work focuses on understanding individual biomechanical responses to footwear and injury using cutting-edge technology.

Research Interests

  • Sex-specific biomechanical responses to advanced footwear technology (AFT)
  • Gait mechanics and injury prevention
  • Individualized sports equipment and clinical interventions

Experience

I have a strong history of securing funding from leading organizations such as Nike and have presented my research at major biomechanics conferences. I also mentor undergraduate researchers and actively collaborate with Dr. Katherine Boyer’s lab at UMass Amherst.

Project Portfolio

  • Sex-Specific Footwear Design – Developed a protocol to assess how shoe designs impact gait biomechanics in men and women.
  • Super Shoe Meta-Analysis – Conducted a comprehensive review of literature on advanced footwear technology and presented findings at an international conference.
  • Data Science in Biomechanics – Utilized Python to analyze gait data, creating predictive models for injury prevention.
