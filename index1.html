Welcome to My Website

I am Justin Matties, a biomechanics researcher and PhD student at UMass Amherst. My research explores the biomechanics of running, focusing on sex-specific responses to advanced footwear technology, injury interventions, and personalized equipment design.

With experience in data science and computational statistics, I integrate advanced tools to analyze gait mechanics and their effects on performance, pain reduction, and clinical outcomes.

Research

My master's research investigated the biomechanical and physiological effects of advanced footwear technology ("super shoes") on male and female runners. This work has been presented at major conferences and published in peer-reviewed journals.

Current Projects

  • Exploring sex-specific biomechanical responses to variable-pressure footwear and injury interventions.
  • Integrating computational tools for gait analysis and injury prevention strategies.
