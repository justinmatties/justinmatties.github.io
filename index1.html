Welcome to My Website

I am Justin Matties, a biomechanics researcher and PhD student at UMass Amherst. My work focuses on understanding the biomechanics of running, exploring sex-specific responses to advanced footwear, injury interventions, and personalized equipment design.

With expertise in biomechanics, data science, and computational statistics, I integrate advanced tools to analyze gait mechanics and their effects on performance, pain reduction, and clinical outcomes.

Research

Master's Thesis Research

Investigated the biomechanical and physiological effects of advanced footwear technology ("super shoes") on distance running performance and training adaptations. Presented findings at international conferences and published in peer-reviewed journals.

PhD Research Focus

Exploring sex-specific biomechanical responses to footwear and injury interventions, with a focus on individualized solutions to improve performance and reduce injury-related pain.
