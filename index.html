About Me

I am an applied biomechanics consultant and PhD student at the University of Massachusetts. My research focuses on understanding individual biomechanical responses to footwear, injuries, and interventions using advanced tools from biomechanics and data science. I aim to improve athletic performance and minimize injury-related pain through innovative solutions tailored to individual needs.

Research Highlights

Master's Thesis Research

My master's research examined the **biomechanical and physiological effects of advanced footwear technology (AFT)** on distance runners. This work analyzed differences in biomechanical responses between male and female runners to provide sex-specific insights into how carbon-fiber plate shoes impact running economy and performance. Results from this research were presented at major biomechanics conferences and led to publications in peer-reviewed journals.

PhD Research Focus

My current PhD research continues this line of inquiry by exploring sex-specific and individual responses to footwear using cutting-edge tools in biomechanics. A key question is how gait mechanics change in response to external interventions and how these changes vary across populations, with the ultimate goal of designing individualized solutions for performance and injury prevention.
