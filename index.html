Biomechanics research focused on footwear, human movement, and applied performance science.

I am Justin Matties, MS, a PhD student in Kinesiology at UMass Amherst working at the intersection of biomechanics, movement science, and data-driven research. My work centers on how individuals respond to footwear and training interventions, with an emphasis on clean study design, practical interpretation, and translational impact.

Focus Areas

Research themes

Footwear Biomechanics

Examining how runners respond mechanically and energetically to footwear design, cushioning, and long-term adaptation.

Human Movement

Studying gait, performance, and variability to better understand individualized movement responses in sport and clinical contexts.

Applied Data Science

Using reproducible analysis, computational tools, and careful interpretation to connect biomechanics data with meaningful research questions.

Background

Built around research, teaching, and translational communication.

Alongside ongoing doctoral work, I have served in research and teaching roles, contributed to biomechanics-focused lab environments, and developed a professional path grounded in both rigorous analysis and clear communication.
